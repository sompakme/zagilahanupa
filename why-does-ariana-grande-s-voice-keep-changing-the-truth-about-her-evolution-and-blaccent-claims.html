Ariana Grande Was Slammed For Her Confusing Voice Changes During Interviews

In 2010, Ariana Grande captured public attention through her role as Cat Valentine on Nickelodeon's Victorious. Known for her vibrant red hair, soulful ballads, and trademark high-pitched voice, her unique speaking voice left a lasting impression on her fans.

However, a TikTok video went viral featuring a compilation of interview clips with Grande’s changing voice spanning from 2013 to 2022. Although her Cat Valentine voice was a character choice, some interviews from 2013 showed her speaking in a similar manner.

As time passed, distinct changes in her voice became evident. By 2014, Grande's voice had adopted a lower, raspier tone, which persisted through 2016 and 2017. The most significant shift occurred in 2018 when her voice seemed to transform the most. While it's natural for voices to evolve over nine years, many TikTok users commented not only on the pitch alterations but also on changes in her accent, particularly referencing a 2018 interview.

Nevertheless, keen observers noticed differences in her speaking voice in a video from her 2019 appearance on the Zach Sang Show. The footage sparked confusion among viewers that led to a Reddit discussion about her changing voice, with some noting that her voice seems to change in each new video they encounter.

Ariana Grande's Changing Voice Is Allegedly Due To The Chameleon Effect

Ariana Grande shared a question-and-answer video for her beauty brand, R.E.M on Youtube, but fans were quick to notice a significant change in her voice since filming for Wicked.

Normally known for her relatively deep voice with an American accent, in the new video, her voice sounded noticeably higher, softer, and had a touch of a European twang, resembling her Wicked character, Glinda. Some people have previously observed that she tends to mirror the voices of those around her, which might explain the difference.

In the 11-minute YouTube clip, Grande addressed questions from fans, including her role as Glinda in the upcoming Wicked movie. Social media users were quick to comment on the perceived change in her voice. One commented, "Why does ariana grande have a new voice or accent every year?" while the other one wrote, "lol the three different voices in one video."

Amid criticism over her new accent, some fans defended the singer, stating that she sounds normal and that people shouldn't be so concerned. Others suggested that she might be subconsciously adopting the accents of those she interacts with, a phenomenon known as the Chameleon Effect.

As per a study in the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, the Chameleon Effect pertains to the unconscious mimicry of the postures, mannerisms, facial expressions, and other behaviors exhibited by one's interaction partners. This leads to a passive and unintentional adjustment of one's own behavior to align with that of others in their present social setting.

Despite differing opinions, one thing is clear – Grande's voice has sparked quite a bit of confusion and conversation.
