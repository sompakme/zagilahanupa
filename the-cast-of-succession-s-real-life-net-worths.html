HBO's Emmy-sweeping drama Succession draws viewers into a world of Shakespearean family drama, corporate backstabbing, and the obscenely opulent life of the Roy family.

Led by the media titan Logan Roy (played by Brian Cox) and his dysfunctional offspring, the Roys battle for power, control, and immense wealth.

Since its premiere in 2018, Succession has emerged as a sensation, earning accolades from both fans and critics. With 13 Emmys, five Golden Globes, and five Critics' Choice awards, Succession firmly cements its place in the hall of fame of critically acclaimed television shows.

The cast of Succession has not only won over millions of audiences with their gripping performances but also boasts net worths that reflect the opulence depicted in the hit television series.

The Succession cast's salaries are just as dramatic as the plotlines. According to Deadline Hollywood, Jeremy Strong (Kendall Roy), Sarah Snook (Siobhan Roy), Kieran Culkin (Roman Roy), and some other season regulars renewed their contracts to earn at minimum $350,000 per episode, while Matthew Macfadyen (Tom Wambsgans) and Nicholas Braun (Greg Hirsch) trail slightly behind at $300,000 each.

8 Nicholas Braun

Nicholas Braun's Estimated Net Worth - $4 Million

Nicholas Braun, aka "Cousin Greg," has won hearts with his endearing awkwardness on Succession.

While Greg might still be navigating the Roy family minefield, Braun's career has been far from uncertain. Before Succession, he built his resume with notable roles in films like The Perks Of Being A Wallflower and The Stanford Prison Experiment.

This early success translated into a comfortable, if not extravagant, salary in Succession's early seasons. However, alongside his fellow castmates, Braun's renegotiation before season three catapulted him into a new financial league.

Braun now commands a princely sum of over $300,000 per episode, a testament to his growing influence and the show's immense popularity.

As of 2024, Nicholas Braun's net worth is estimated at around $4 million.

7 Sarah Snook

Sarah Snook's Estimated Net Worth - $4 Million

Sarah Snook kick-started her acting journey with short films like Crystal Jam and The Best Man, before transitioning to television.

In 2010 she was cast as Siobhan “Shiv” Roy in Succession, earning acclaim that culminated in a Primetime Emmy Award for "Outstanding Supporting Actress" in 2020.

With a diverse filmography spanning Australian indie darlings like Predestination and Hollywood blockbusters like Steve Jobs, Snook has proven her versatility and talent.

The Emmy Award winner has consistently picked quality projects over quick paydays, and her dedication to her craft shines through in every performance.

Snook commands $300,000 to $350,000 per episode to play Shiv, which has evidently helped her amass a net worth estimated in the range of $4 million.

In the first two seasons of Succession, Snook earned $100,000 per episode, totaling $1 million per season and $2 million in total. For the third season, her salary was bumped to $350,000 per episode, or $3.5 million for the season.

6 Kieran Culkin

Kieran Culkin's Estimated Net Worth - $5 Million

Kieran Culkin has successfully pitched his tent away from the Culkin family limelight.

The Emmy Award winner has quietly made his mark in Hollywood, stealing scenes in classics like Igby Goes Down, Scott Pilgrim vs. The World, and Home Alone, where he proved his acting chops from a young age.

His turn as Roman Roy in Succession might be his latest breakout, with a hefty per-episode paycheck and awards buzz, but Culkin's success is built on years of consistent work and undeniable talent.

In the first two seasons of Succession, Culkin, alongside the main cast, earned $100,000 per episode, culminating in $2 million for the 20 episodes of the first and second seasons.

Before production for the third season was announced, Kieran and the show's regular cast renegotiated with the producers and secured a notable increase to $350,000 per episode, summing up to $3.5 million for the season.

Today, Kieran Culkin's net worth is estimated at $5 million.

5 Matthew Macfadyen

Matthew Macfadyen's Estimated Net Worth - $5 Million

For some, Tom Wambsgans is the ultimate company man, a loyal but hapless pawn in the Roy family chess game.

Yet, Matthew Macfadyen, the man behind the Wambsgans veneer, is anything but a one-dimensional actor.

A chameleon of sorts, Macfadyen has charmed audiences with his role in Pride & Prejudice and his chilling portrayal of MI6 officer Thomas Archer in Spooks.

His early film roles in Enigma and In My Father's Den brought him recognition in the industry before he earned global prominence as Mr. Darcy in Pride & Prejudice.

This impressive filmography, bolstered by his award-winning turn in Succession, has seen Macfadyen accumulate an estimated net worth of $5 million.

His nuanced portrayal of Wambsgans, a character teetering between pathetic and strangely endearing, earned him a well-deserved Emmy Award.

And with a renegotiated salary of around $300,000 per episode, his financial future is as secure as Tom's grip on Shiv's arm.

4 Jeremy Strong

Jeremy Strong's Estimated Net Worth - $6 Million

Jeremy Strong boasts an impressive resume in his two decades in the film industry.

Strong has effortlessly navigated Hollywood, starring in critically acclaimed films like The Big Short, Selma, and Zero Dark Thirty. His Oscar-nominated turn in The Trial Of The Chicago 7 highlighted his comedic timing and versatility.

He had recurring roles on television in The Good Wife and Masters Of Sex. Strong had his breakthrough in 2018 with the role of media conglomerate heir Kendall Roy in Succession, for which he won a Primetime Emmy Award in 2020 and a Golden Globe Award in 2022.

3 Fisher Stevens

Fisher Stevens' Net Worth - $8 Million

While he might be a newcomer to the Roy family feud in Succession, Fisher Stevens is a Hollywood veteran with an esimated $8 million net worth – built through an eclectic career spanning acting, directing, producing, and writing.

Long before navigating the shark-infested waters of Waystar Royco, Stevens made his mark in classics like Short Circuit and Super Mario Bros. Stevens cut his teeth in horror films like The Burning before landing a starring role in the popular daytime soap opera Ryan's Hope.

From guest appearances on Law & Order: SVU to recurring roles in The Blacklist and The Good Fight, Stevens has become a familiar face on television.

He directed and produced several projects, including the chilling thriller The Midnight Meat Train and the short-lived sci-fi series Early Editions.

His crowning achievement came when he won an Oscar in the "Best Documentary" category for The Cove, an exposé on dolphin hunting in Japan.

His character in Succession might just be getting his bearings —but Fisher Stevens is far from a newbie regarding experience and net worth.

2 Alan Ruck

Alan Ruck's Estimated Net Worth - $12 Million

Alan Ruck came to prominence for playing Cameron Frye in the 1986 John Hughes comedy film Ferris Bueller's Day Off, but he's also one of the richest cast members of Succession.

Ruck's net worth is estimated to be $12 million, according to various sources. Ruck's other credits include roles in films like Bad Boys, Speed, Twister, and Freaky.

Ruck ventured into television, appearing in series like Going Places, Muscle, and The Exorcist. It was his role as Connor Roy in Succession that paid off.

Like all the actors playing Roy's children in the series, Ruck renegotiated a salary bump when season three kicked off, bringing home between $300,000 and $350,000 per episode.

From Ferris Bueller to Succession, Alan Ruck has built fortune through four decades of stellar projects.

1 Brian Cox

Brian Cox's Estimated Net Worth - $15 Million

Coincidentally, the wealthiest character on Succession is played by the purported richest actor in the cast, Brian Cox. This makes him the wealthiest member of the Succession cast, and for good reason.
