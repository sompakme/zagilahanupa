From his iconic roles in Mrs. Doubtfire and Dead Poets Society to his hilarious stand-up and television appearances, Williams was a master of both humor and drama. Yet, while most fans knew him as the funny, larger-than-life character they saw on screen, there was so much more to him than what was portrayed.

Through his movies, Williams revealed aspects of his personality that many had no idea about. Let's explore some of these lesser-known parts of Robin Williams' personality that were revealed through his movies.

9 How Robin Williams Began His Journey To Fame

In 1976, Robin Williams began performing stand-up comedy in San Francisco Bay. His first performance was at the Holy City Zoo, a comedy club in San Francisco where he worked his way up from bartending. He relocated to Los Angeles and continued playing stand-up in bars until 1977 when TV producer George Schlatter asked him to appear on his show "Laugh-in," which aired the same year and was his first TV appearance.

Williams rose to stardom thanks to his iconic part in the Mork & Mindy TV series, and he continued to shine by acting in a number of different movies. For his role in Good Will Hunting, Williams won the Oscar Award for Best Supporting Actor. He's also won two Emmys, four Golden Globes, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, and five Grammys.

8 Who Is Robin Williams Familly?

His first marriage was to Valeria Velardi, in June 1978. Williams met her while working as a bartender in San Francisco. They had a son, Zachary Pym "Zak," together. Williams and Velardi separated in 1988 after he had an affair with their son's nanny Marsha Garces.

Williams married Garces in April 1989, while she was six months pregnant with his child. Zelda Rae and Alan were Garces and Williams' two children. Garces and Williams separated in 2008. Williams then married Susan Schneider in October 2011, however, they never had any children together.

7 Inside Robin Williams's Tragic Passing

The world was shocked to learn in August 2014 that Robin Williams had ended his life in his home in Tiburon, California. Following his death, it was found out that he had been diagnosed with Parkinson's-like symptoms. Although, according to The Daily Mail, his autopsy later indicated that he had undiagnosed, severe Lewy body disease that had spread throughout his brain causing him to suffer from dementia.

6 What Has One Hour Photo Revealed About Robin Williams?

Robin Williams played Seymour "Sy" Parrish, a photo technician at SavMart's one-hour picture development clinic. He lives alone outside the department store. Every day, he works hard to guarantee that his customers receive the highest-quality images available. He had no one and nothing to come home to at the end of the day and his work is his life. He spends his evenings alone in his desolate living room, watching television.

One Hour Photo featured a lot of lonely moments for Sy, Williams' character which resonated with Williams who grew up in a wealthy home but, experienced loneliness from a very young age.

5 What Patch Adams Revealed About Robin Williams's Real Life

Robin Williams played Hunter Patch Adams in this comic drama, who decides to become a doctor after dealing with depression in a mental hospital. While at the mental hospital, he finds that employing comedy instead of doctor-centered psychotherapy benefits the patients and gives them a new sense of purpose in life.

Hunter then attends Virginia Medical University but is disappointed with the school's clinical approach to patient care too and decided to establish his own medical clinic for those without insurance with the help of his wealthy friend. Before a tragedy forces Adams to reconsider his approach, he builds a special bond with one of his fellow medical students.

4 What Mrs. Doubtfire Revealed About Robin Williams's Personality

Robin Williams acted as divorced Daniel Hillard, who is concerned that he doesn't spend enough time with his kids, and comes up with a complex scheme. He disguises himself as an older British woman and persuades his ex-wife, Miranda, with the aid of his artistic brother Frank, to engage him as a nanny. Mrs. Doubtfire charms the kids and makes Daniel a better parent, but when he has to attend separate meetings at the same restaurant with both Daniel and his nanny image, his secret was revealed.

As Robin Williams had previously experienced divorce, he could certainly relate to this film, and it also revealed his loving family-oriented side. There is no question that he infused his personality and life experiences into his performance.

3 What Has The Fisher King Revealed About Robin Williams?

The Fisher King is a 1991 American fantasy comedy-drama starring Robin Williams. The film tells the story of a radio shock jock who tries to find redemption by helping a man whose life he inadvertently shattered. The film depicts a radio shock jock, who provokes a caller into taking out a group of innocent people in a Manhattan bar, he then becomes depressed and turns to the bottle. "The Fisher King" depicts sensitive subjects such as depression and mental illness.

Williams definitely related to those subjects as he himself had his own experiences with dealing with depression, alcoholism, and even addiction.

2 What Has Good Morning, Vietnam Revealed About Robin Williams?

Good Morning, Vietnam is a 1987 American war comedy film where Robin Williams acted as a radio funny-man Adrian Cronauer who was sent to Vietnam to bring a little comic relief to the lives of the soldiers. Upon his arrival, Cronauer quickly endears himself to the G.I.s with his irreverent take on the war, but his superior officer, Sergeant Major Dickerson is less than thrilled. As Dickerson attempts to reign in Cronauer's broadcasts, the radio host finds himself drawn to a local Vietnamese girl named Trinh.

Good Morning, Vietnam showcased and highlighted the incomparable talents of Robin Williams, from his remarkable impressions and improvisational genius to his deeply compassionate nature. This classic movie allowed us to appreciate the full range of Williams' remarkable abilities and truly experience the power of his art.

1 What Dead Poets Society Revealed About Robin Williams's Personality

Dead Poets Society is a 1989 American drama film that starred Robin Williams as John Keating who is a new English teacher at an all-boys preparatory school with a long history of tradition and excellence. He uses unconventional methods to connect with his students, who are under intense pressure from their parents and the school.

With the help of Keating, Neil Perry, Todd Anderson and others learn to break free from their inhibitions. Together, they discover the importance of living for the moment and the power of making dreams a reality.

Williams said, "I loved school, maybe too much really. I was summa cum laude in high school. I was driven that way. I wasn't...exuberant," he said. "I spent about three years in an all-boys school. It was almost like the one in Dead Poets Society. Blazer. Latin motto. I was getting pushed around a lot. Not only was there like physical bullying, but there was intellectual bullying going on. It made me toughen up, but it also made me pull back a lot. I had a certain reticence about dealing with people. Through comedy, I found a way to bridge the gap."
