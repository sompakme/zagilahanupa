Early Life and Real Estate Career

Donald Sterling, born Donald Tokowitz, was born in Chicago in 1934 to Jewish immigrants. His family moved to Los Angeles when he was a child, and he attended Theodore Roosevelt High School. In 1961, Sterling began his career as a divorce and personal injury attorney.

He started investing in real estate and purchased his first property, a 26-unit apartment building in Beverly Hills. Over the years, Sterling acquired numerous properties in Los Angeles, including the Sterling Plaza in Beverly Hills. As of April 2014, he owned 162 properties in Los Angeles.

Sterling’s success in the real estate industry allowed him to amass a significant portfolio of properties throughout Los Angeles. His investments included residential and commercial properties, and he became known for his lucrative deals and acquisitions.

Ownership of the Los Angeles Clippers

In 1981, Donald Sterling purchased the San Diego Clippers for $12.5 million. He then relocated the team to Los Angeles against the NBA’s wishes in 1984. Throughout his ownership, the Clippers faced numerous challenges and had a prolonged period of struggle, failing to achieve a winning season until 1991-1992. Despite criticism and being labeled as one of the worst owners in sports, Sterling defiantly held ownership of the team for over three decades.

As of 2013, the Clippers were valued at $700 million, representing substantial growth since Sterling’s initial acquisition. Racist Controversy and Team Sale

In April 2014, the NBA was engulfed in the Donald Sterling controversy. Private recordings of Sterling making racist comments were leaked to the public, sparking outrage and condemnation. The NBA wasted no time in responding to the situation, taking swift action against Sterling.

As a direct consequence of his remarks, the NBA banned Sterling from the league for life and fined him $2.5 million. Additionally, Sterling was stripped of most of his authority over the Los Angeles Clippers, the team he had owned for over three decades. This scandal not only tarnished Sterling’s reputation but also raised serious questions about racial equality within the NBA and the sports industry as a whole.

“We stand together in condemning Mr. Sterling’s views.” – NBA Commissioner Adam Silver

The aftermath of the controversy led to significant changes within the Clippers organization. Sterling’s wife, Shelly Sterling, played a pivotal role in resolving the situation. She negotiated the sale of the team to Steve Ballmer, the former CEO of Microsoft, for a staggering $2 billion. This marked the highest price ever paid for an NBA franchise.

Ballmer’s acquisition of the team brought a fresh start for the Clippers and signaled a new era for the franchise. With the controversy surrounding Sterling behind them, the team could focus on rebuilding their image and pursuing success on and off the court.

Despite the controversy and the subsequent sale of the Clippers, the impact of this racist controversy lingers, serving as a reminder of the ongoing challenges society faces in combating discrimination and promoting inclusivity in sports and beyond.
