Which is correct caldron or cauldron?

A cauldron (or caldron) is a large pot (kettle) for cooking or boiling over an open fire, with a lid and frequently with an arc-shaped hanger. In other words, a large kettle.

Is cauldron a real word?

The cauldron, while conjuring up images of — well, conjuring — actually came from the Latin root calidus, meaning “hot.” We can see this root in other Latin-based languages, including the Spanish caliente and Italian caldo. In English, the word became cauldron — a creepy name for a very useful pot.

What does caldron mean?

1 : a large kettle or boiler. 2 : something resembling a boiling cauldron in intensity or degree of agitation a cauldron of intense emotions.

What is the opposite of a cauldron?

The word cauldron refers to a large, metal cooking pot that is used over an open fire. There are no categorical antonyms for this word.

What are witch brooms?
